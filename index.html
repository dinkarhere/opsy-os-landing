01Invoicing

Not accounting software. An invoicing app you'll actually want to open.

Live-preview editor — form on the left, brand-styled invoice on the right, updating as you type. Gapless per-year numbering assigned atomically at finalize. Draft → Sent → Partial → Paid lifecycle. Receipts track partial payments, so balance is always computed, never wrongly stored.

02Payments

₹ and UPI, first-class.

The client portal shows a UPI QR code, your UPI ID, and bank details. No gateway, no cut — clients pay you directly and you log the receipt. One share link per client. No accounts to create, no passwords to reset.

03The OS layer

Not just invoices. The whole practice.

Clients from lead to closed. Projects with statuses and retainers. Tasks with priorities and due dates. Notion-style notes with icons. A dashboard for revenue, outstanding balance, and what's been sent this month.

04Keyboard-first

⌘K to anywhere. Space to create.

Command palette jumps to any client, project, invoice, or note. On any list, Space means "new ___" — context-aware. ? opens help wherever you are. Dark-only, hairline borders, made for people who live at the keyboard.

05Security

Your workspace is your workspace.

Each account gets its own tenant. Isolation enforced by Firestore security rules — not just filtered in the UI. Client portal reads are ownership-checked at the query and again at render. Google sign-in via Firebase Auth; the portal is token-based, no client accounts to manage.

Positioning

And a few things Opsy OS isn't.

01

Not accounting software.

No ledgers, journals, GST returns, or TDS. If you need those, keep your accountant.

02

Not a payment processor.

You keep 100%. Clients pay you directly over UPI or bank; you log the receipt.

03

Not a CRM.

A light client model — leads, negotiations, active, closed — not a sales pipeline with stages and forecasts.

04

Not a team tool.

Single-owner workspaces today. If teams come, they'll come deliberately, without gluing on seat pricing.
